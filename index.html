STEP 02 — ISSUANCE
Federal government issues Section 45Q credits to the project owner

IRS Compliance & Credit Integrity
Seven Layers of Protection Against IRS Scrutiny

The credit holder has built a multi-layer compliance architecture specifically designed to withstand IRS examination. Every layer is independent — meaning no single point of failure can invalidate the credits. This is not a tax strategy or opinion letter. It is a federally registered, audited, and IRS-acknowledged credit program built on hard physical and legal foundations.

01
IRS Pre-Filing Registration
Before any credit transfer, the Credit Holder obtains an IRS pre-filing registration number via the IRS Energy Credits Online portal — a formal acknowledgment that the credits exist, the project qualifies under §45Q, and the transfer is eligible under §6418. Registration number is disclosed to the buyer in due diligence. No registration = no transfer. This is the IRS's own gatekeeping step built into the Inflation Reduction Act.
02
Statutory IRC §45Q Authority
Section 45Q is enacted federal law — not a regulation, not a ruling, not a tax strategy. Congress passed §45Q specifically to incentivize carbon capture and permanent geological sequestration. The credit was enhanced by the Inflation Reduction Act of 2022 and is currently active law. Credits issued under §45Q carry the same legal weight as any other provision of the Internal Revenue Code.
03
Physical Verification & MRV
Carbon sequestration is independently measured, reported, and verified (MRV) by a qualified third-party validator. Deep soil sampling to 60 feet with continuous monitoring confirms permanent storage. Credits are issued only for verified CO₂ removed — not estimated, projected, or offset. Physical proof precedes every credit unit. This is the bedrock of the program's defensibility: the CO₂ is provably in the ground.
04
Annual Independent Audit
The Credit Holder undergoes annual third-party audits of the carbon capture operation, credit issuance process, and compliance with §45Q requirements. Audit reports are available to buyers during due diligence. Auditors verify measurement methodology, storage permanence, and credit quantity. These reports constitute a formal contemporaneous record — exactly the type of documentation the IRS requires in any §45Q examination.
05
Material Participation — Active Credits
The credit originator materially participates in the carbon capture project — a critical structural requirement that causes these to be generated as active federal tax credits under §38, not passive activity credits. This distinction matters enormously: passive credits are subject to §469 limitations and carryforward rules that restrict their use. Active §45Q credits transferred via §6418 face no such restriction and apply directly against any federal income tax liability.
06
Seller Bears Recapture Risk
Under the §6418 transfer structure, recapture risk stays with the Credit Holder, not the buyer. If the IRS were to determine a credit was miscalculated or ineligible after transfer, the recapture obligation falls on the seller — not the transferee. In addition, the Credit Holder contractually indemnifies the buyer against any pre-transfer compliance failures. The buyer's exposure is limited to the purchase price paid — not expanded by downstream audit risk.
07
3× Redundancy Reserve
The Credit Holder maintains triple redundancy reserves — meaning it holds more verified carbon credits than it transfers in any given transaction. If any portion of the transferred credits were challenged, replacement credits of equivalent type and vintage are available. Alternatively, the buyer receives a full refund. This reserve structure means a successful IRS challenge does not result in buyer loss — it results in replacement or return of funds.
